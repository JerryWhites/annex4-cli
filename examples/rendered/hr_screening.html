1.3 Intended purpose

DescriptionTalentFlow ScreenAI automatically screens, ranks, and shortlists job applications for white-collar and technical positions. The system analyses structured applicant data (CV text, application form fields) and produces a ranked shortlist with explanatory score breakdowns for each candidate. The system is designed to assist — not replace — human recruiters in the initial screening phase.
Intended usersHR professionals and recruitment specialists at partner organisations who use TalentFlow ScreenAI as an assistive tool for initial candidate screening. All system outputs require human review before any employment decision is made.
Deployment formSaaS — accessed via web dashboard and REST API
Geographic scopeEuropean Union; not validated for use outside EU member states
Persons affectedNatural persons who submit job applications to organisations using TalentFlow ScreenAI. Applicants are notified of AI-assisted screening in the job posting and application form.

1.4 Foreseeable misuse

  • Automated rejection without human review of individual candidates
  • Use as the sole basis for employment decisions without recruiter oversight
  • Screening on characteristics not listed in the approved feature set
  • Deployment in jurisdictions outside the validated geographic scope

2. Development process Annex IV §2

MethodologySupervised learning using gradient-boosted trees (XGBoost 2.0) trained on six years of historical recruitment data. Feature engineering pipeline extracts structured signals from free-text CV fields via a fine-tuned sentence-transformer (multilingual MiniLM-L12) frozen at inference time. Model selection via 5-fold cross-validation with stratified splits. Final model selected on balanced accuracy across gender subgroups.
ArchitectureTwo-stage pipeline: (1) NLP feature extractor (sentence-transformer, 768-dim embeddings, CPU inference) → (2) XGBoost classifier (500 estimators, max_depth=6, learning_rate=0.05). Output: a continuous score [0, 1] representing screening probability, plus SHAP feature importance breakdown for each prediction. No generative AI components. No real-time learning.
InputsStructured applicant record containing: CV text (free-form, up to 5 000 tokens), application form fields (role applied for, years of experience, highest qualification), and optionally a cover letter. All personal identifiers (name, address, contact details, date of birth) are stripped before the record reaches the model via a PII-removal preprocessor.
OutputsA ranked shortlist of candidates with: screening score [0–100], SHAP feature breakdown (top-5 contributing features per candidate), and a plain-language summary of the main factors. The shortlist is delivered to the recruiter dashboard. No direct communication with applicants.
Pre-trained componentsmultilingual-MiniLM-L12-v2 (sentence-transformers, Apache-2.0 licence). Weights frozen; not fine-tuned on TalentFlow data. Used solely for embedding generation.
Design choices rationaleTree-based model chosen over deep learning to maximise interpretability (SHAP explanations are exact, not approximate) and to avoid hallucination risks of generative models in a high-stakes screening context.

3. Monitoring, functioning and control Annex IV §3 / Article 15

Capabilities and limitationsThe system is validated for screening white-collar and technical roles in EU member states. It is NOT validated for: (a) roles requiring security clearance, (b) senior executive positions, (c) applicants from outside the EU without EU educational credentials, (d) roles with fewer than 10 historical hires in the training data. The system does not make employment decisions — it produces an assistive ranked list requiring human review.
Foreseeable unintended outcomes(1) Perpetuation of historical hiring biases embedded in training data despite mitigation measures. (2) Proxy discrimination via non-protected features that correlate with protected characteristics (e.g., institution names as proxies for socioeconomic background). (3) Over-reliance by recruiters on AI score without reading underlying rationale. (4) Gaming by applicants aware of high-scoring CV patterns. (5) Disparate impact on applicants with non-linear career histories (e.g., career breaks for caregiving) which the model has limited training signal for.
Human oversight measures(1) All shortlisted candidates must be reviewed by a qualified recruiter before invitation to interview. Automatic progression is technically blocked. (2) SHAP explanations are displayed alongside every score to support informed human review. (3) Recruiters can override the system ranking at any time with a mandatory rationale field. (4) AI Governance Officer reviews override rates monthly; spikes trigger model review. (5) All deployer staff with access to the dashboard must complete the mandatory AI literacy training module (min. 2 hours) before activation.
Cybersecurity measuresAll API endpoints authenticated via OAuth 2.0 (client credentials flow). PII removed server-side before model inference (dedicated anonymisation service, pen-tested annually). Model artefacts signed and stored in isolated artifact registry. No model weights exposed via API. SOC 2 Type II audit scheduled Q4 2026.

8. Post-market monitoring plan Article 72

Monitoring approachMonthly evaluation of balanced accuracy and fairness metrics on a live-labelled holdout sample (rolling 90-day window, n ≈ 8 000 records per month). Drift detection via Population Stability Index (PSI) on top-20 input features. Quarterly review by AI Governance Committee with mandatory corrective action trigger if any KPI breaches its threshold.
Drift detection methodPopulation Stability Index (PSI) computed weekly on the distribution of the top-20 SHAP features. Alert threshold PSI > 0.20; model review triggered at PSI > 0.25.
Update trigger thresholdsAny KPI breach sustained over 2 consecutive monthly evaluations triggers mandatory model review. A breach of the fairness KPIs triggers an emergency governance review within 14 days.
Responsible personJan Kovář, Head of AI Governance (jan.kovar@talentflow-ai.de)
Review frequencyMonthly KPI dashboard; quarterly full governance review

Key performance indicators

  • Balanced accuracy ≥ 0.82 (aggregate)
  • Fairness delta (gender) ≤ ±5 pp vs aggregate
  • Fairness delta (age 50+) ≤ ±5 pp vs aggregate
  • False negative rate ≤ 0.12
  • PSI (input features) < 0.20 (alert) / < 0.25 (model review)
  • Recruiter override rate < 15% (trigger: governance review)
