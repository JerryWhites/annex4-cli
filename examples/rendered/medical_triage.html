1.3 Intended purpose

DescriptionMedTriage Assist is an AI-assisted clinical decision support tool for emergency department triage nurses. The system analyses structured patient data (vital signs, chief complaint, triage assessment) and proposes a Manchester Triage System (MTS) category [1–5] to support — not replace — the triage nurse's clinical judgment. The system is a software medical device under Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR) and has been CE-marked as a Class IIa device.
Intended usersRegistered triage nurses and emergency physicians at hospitals using the integrated emergency department information system (EDIS). Clinical training on the system required before activation. All triage decisions remain the clinical responsibility of the healthcare professional.
Deployment formEmbedded module within the hospital EDIS; displayed on triage workstation. Not accessible via consumer-facing interfaces. Not a standalone application.
Geographic scopeEU member states with validated language support — DE, FR, NL, IT, ES
Persons affectedPatients presenting to emergency departments at hospitals using MedTriage Assist. Patients or their representatives are informed of AI-assisted triage via admission documentation.

1.4 Foreseeable misuse

  • Delegation of triage decision to the AI without clinical nurse review
  • Use outside emergency department triage context (e.g., ward prioritisation)
  • Use for paediatric patients without the separately validated paediatric module
  • Deployment in languages outside the validated five-language scope

2. Development process Annex IV §2

MethodologyMultimodal neural network trained on retrospective triage records from 14 EU emergency departments (2019–2024). Text encoder (ClinicalBERT, multilingual, fine-tuned) processes chief complaint; structured vital sign encoder (two-layer MLP) processes numeric clinical parameters. Late fusion via attention mechanism. Class-weighted cross-entropy loss to address class imbalance (MTS 1–2 cases rare).
ArchitectureInput: chief complaint text (max 512 tokens) + structured vital signs (12 fields). Text branch: ClinicalBERT-multilingual (110M params, fine-tuned 3 epochs). Vitals branch: 2-layer MLP [12 → 64 → 32]. Fusion: cross-attention [32 → 32]. Output head: softmax over 5 MTS categories. Inference: CPU, < 200ms per prediction. Model frozen at deployment; no federated or online learning.
Inputs(1) Chief complaint: free-text entered by triage nurse (5–200 words, 5 validated languages). (2) Vital signs: heart rate, blood pressure (systolic/diastolic), SpO2, respiratory rate, temperature, Glasgow Coma Scale, pain scale [0–10], age (categorical), sex at birth. No imaging, no laboratory results, no patient history data used at this stage.
OutputsProposed MTS triage category [1 = Immediate, 2 = Very Urgent, 3 = Urgent, 4 = Standard, 5 = Non-Urgent] with confidence score. Top-3 reasoning factors displayed in plain clinical language. The proposal is displayed alongside the MTS decision flowchart; the nurse selects the final category independently.
Pre-trained componentsClinicalBERT-multilingual (Hugging Face, Apache-2.0). Fine-tuned on MedAI retrospective data with full weight updates. Original pre-training data: PubMed, MIMIC-III (de-identified), multilingual clinical notes corpus.
Design choices rationaleLate fusion (rather than early fusion) chosen to preserve interpretability of each modality independently, enabling clinical validation of the text vs vitals contribution to the prediction. Attention-based fusion makes cross-modal reasoning auditable by clinical AI experts.

3. Monitoring, functioning and control Annex IV §3 / Article 15

Capabilities and limitationsValidated for adult patients (≥ 18) presenting to emergency departments in EU EDs with validated language support. NOT for: paediatric patients (separate module required), psychiatric emergencies (insufficient training data), patients unable to provide chief complaint, out-of-hospital triage, or languages beyond the 5 validated. Accuracy decreases for rare presentations (< 0.5% of training data).
Foreseeable unintended outcomes(1) Under-triage for elderly frail patients — structurally higher rate documented; mitigated by mandatory senior nurse review protocol. (2) Automation bias — nurses anchoring on AI proposal rather than independent assessment. (3) Atypical presentations (NSTEMI without chest pain, stroke without focal deficit) may be under-triaged if chief complaint does not reflect acuity. (4) Language model hallucination risk for rare clinical terms not in training vocabulary — mitigation: confidence threshold filter (proposals below 0.60 confidence suppressed).
Human oversight measures(1) AI proposal is advisory only — nurse selects final MTS category independently. The system cannot submit a triage category; only the nurse can confirm. (2) Proposals with confidence < 0.60 are not displayed; nurse triages without AI input. (3) All AI proposals and final nurse decisions logged; discordance > 1 MTS level flagged for clinical lead review within 24 hours. (4) Mandatory 4-hour competency training for all triage staff before system activation. (5) Monthly discordance report reviewed by Chief Medical Officer.
Cybersecurity measuresMDR-compliant cybersecurity controls (ENISA medical device cybersecurity guidance). EDIS integration via HL7 FHIR R4 over TLS 1.3. No patient data leaves hospital network. Model inference runs on-premises (hospital server; model weights not accessible via API). Annual cybersecurity audit by NL-certified MedTech auditor.

8. Post-market monitoring plan Article 72

Monitoring approachPost-Market Clinical Follow-Up (PMCF) per MDCG 2020-7 and MDR Annex XIV. Monthly discordance analysis (AI proposal vs nurse decision vs final clinical outcome). Quarterly under-triage rate evaluation against 5% threshold. Annual PMCF report submitted to BSI Group. Serious incident reporting per MDR Article 87 and EU AI Act Article 73.
Drift detection methodMonthly distribution monitoring on vital sign features (PSI). Quarterly re-validation on rolling prospective cohort (n ≈ 5 000/quarter per language).
Update trigger thresholdsUnder-triage rate > 5% at aggregate OR > 7% for any subgroup → clinical hold and emergency Medical Advisory Board review. Category agreement drop > 0.03 → mandatory re-evaluation before next deployment.
Responsible personDr. Lena Fischer, CMO (l.fischer@medai-diagnostics.ch)
Review frequencyMonthly PMCF dashboard; quarterly review; annual PMCF report

Key performance indicators

  • Under-triage rate < 5% (aggregate) — monthly
  • Under-triage rate < 7% for any subgroup — monthly
  • Exact category agreement ≥ 0.75 (aggregate) — quarterly
  • Nurse discordance rate 8–20% (too low = automation bias; too high = clinical concern)
  • Confidence suppression rate < 15% (predictions below 0.60 threshold) — monthly
  • Italian exact agreement ≥ 0.76 — quarterly (improvement target)
