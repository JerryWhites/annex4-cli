1.3 Intended purpose

DescriptionCreditSense Pro assesses the creditworthiness of natural persons applying for retail credit products (personal loans, credit cards, mortgages) at partner financial institutions. The system produces a credit score [0–1000] and a risk tier (A–E). Loan officers use the output as one input in the credit decision process — it does not make or automate credit decisions.
Intended usersLoan officers and credit analysts at partner financial institutions. All credit decisions require human review.
Deployment formAPI integration with partner banking core systems; web dashboard
Geographic scopeEU member states — validated on data from DE, NL, FR, BE, AT
Persons affectedEU natural persons applying for retail credit at institutions using CreditSense Pro. Applicants are informed of AI-assisted assessment in pre-contractual documents.

1.4 Foreseeable misuse

  • Automated rejection without loan officer review
  • Use outside validated product types (e.g., commercial credit, SME lending)
  • Deployment in member states outside validated scope
  • Use without providing applicants the right to human review (GDPR Art. 22)

2. Development process Annex IV §2

MethodologyEnsemble model: logistic regression (interpretability) + LightGBM (predictive lift) combined via stacking. Trained on 36 months of credit performance panel data. Separate sub-models per product type (personal loan, credit card, mortgage) routed by a product classifier. No online learning; all models stateless.
ArchitectureThree-stage pipeline: (1) product classifier → (2) product-specific LightGBM/LR ensemble producing probability of default (12-month horizon) → (3) score calibration layer mapping to [0–1000] scale and A–E risk tier. All components versioned and immutable at inference time.
InputsCredit bureau data (payment history 36 months, utilisation, account count, derogatory marks), application data (amount, term, purpose), and verified income. No behavioural, social media, or non-financial alternative data. PII stripped before model inference.
OutputsCredit score [0–1000], risk tier (A–E), 12-month probability of default, top-5 contributing factors in plain language for regulatory disclosure.
Pre-trained componentsNone.
Design choices rationaleLogistic regression component maintained for EBA internal model governance compliance (interpretability requirement). LightGBM adds ~0.03 AUC lift.

3. Monitoring, functioning and control Annex IV §3 / Article 15

Capabilities and limitationsValidated for retail credit in DE, NL, FR, BE, AT. NOT for: commercial credit, SME lending, investment products, applicants with < 6 months credit history, or member states outside validated scope. Not the sole basis for credit decisions.
Foreseeable unintended outcomes(1) Over-reliance by loan officers on score without reading contributing factors. (2) Structural disadvantage for young applicants lacking credit history. (3) Temporal drift under macroeconomic regime change (post-2025 conditions). (4) Feedback loops: declined applicants cannot build credit history.
Human oversight measures(1) Loan officer sign-off required for all decisions above €500; automated approval/rejection technically blocked. (2) GDPR Art. 22 right to human review implemented with automated disclosure letter. (3) Top-5 contributing factors displayed to loan officer. (4) Override rate monitored monthly.
Cybersecurity measuresISO 27001-certified environment. mTLS API authentication. AES-256 encryption at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit. Air-gapped model registry. Annual pen testing. GDPR DPIA completed and filed with DPA Netherlands.
